Tales of a Professional Armchair Historian

When I was an academic, my focus was eighteenth century British social history, in particular The Grand Tour.
My Master's theis posited that 'in both the experience of the Grand Tour as lived by participants and the perception of the Tour at home it is possible to see the echoes of larger issues in eighteenth-century British society,particularly those of national identity, consumption, luxury and masculinity.'
